Appendix A Trigonometric Identities

It follows from the definitions of sine and cosine

and the Pythagorean theorem that

\begin{equation} \sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) \equiv 1.\tag{A.0.1} \end{equation}

Whenever \(\sec(\theta)\) is defined, i.e. \(\cos(\theta) \neq 0\text{,}\) by dividing \(\cos^2(\theta)\) on both sides of (A.0.1), we obtain another form of the identity

\begin{equation} \tan^2(\theta) + 1 \equiv \sec^2(\theta)\tag{A.0.2} \end{equation}

Also clear from the definitions are that sine is odd and cosine is even, that is

\begin{equation} \sin(-\theta) \equiv -\sin(\theta)\ \text{and}\ \cos(-\theta) \equiv \cos(\theta)\tag{A.0.3} \end{equation}

Other fundamental relationships between trigonometric functions are succinctly expressed by the Euler formula:

\begin{equation} e^{i\theta} = \cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta) \quad (\theta \in \mathbb{R})\tag{A.0.4} \end{equation}

where \(i \in \mathbb{C}\) is a complex number with \(i^2 = -1\text{.}\) For instance,

\begin{align*} \cos(A+B) & + i\sin(A+B) = e^{i(A+B)} = e^{iA}e^{iB}\\ &= (\cos(A) + i\sin(A))(\cos(B) + i\sin(B))\\ &= \cos(A)\cos(B)+i^2\sin(A)\sin(B)+i(\sin(A)\cos(B)+\sin(B)\cos(A)).\\ &=\cos(A)\cos(B)-\sin(A)\sin(B) +i(\sin(A)\cos(B)+\sin(B)\cos(A)). \end{align*}

By comparing the real part and the imaginary part of both sides, we arrive to the following two trigonometric identities.

\begin{gather} \sin(A+B) \equiv \sin(A)\cos(B)+\sin(B)\cos(A)\tag{A.0.5}\\ \cos(A+B) \equiv \cos(A)\cos(B)-\sin(A)\sin(B)\tag{A.0.6} \end{gather}

Replacing \(B\) by \(-B\) and using (A.0.3) we obtain

\begin{equation} \sin(A-B) \equiv \sin(A)\cos(B)-\sin(B)\cos(A)\tag{A.0.7} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \cos(A-B) \equiv \cos(A)\cos(B)+\sin(A)\sin(B)\tag{A.0.8} \end{equation}

Halving the sum of (A.0.5) and (A.0.7) yields an identity relating sum and product of sines

\begin{equation} \frac{1}{2}(\sin(A+B) + \sin(A-B)) \equiv \sin(A)\cos(B).\tag{A.0.9} \end{equation}

Likewise, from (A.0.6) and (A.0.8) we deduce

\begin{equation} \frac{1}{2}(\cos(A+B) + \cos(A-B)) \equiv \cos(A)\cos(B)\tag{A.0.10} \end{equation}
\begin{equation} \frac{1}{2}(\cos(A-B) - \cos(A+B)) \equiv \sin(A)\sin(B)\tag{A.0.11} \end{equation}

By setting \(B=A\) in (A.0.5), we get

\begin{equation} \sin(2A) \equiv 2\sin(A)\cos(A).\tag{A.0.12} \end{equation}

Similarly, we deduce from (A.0.6) and (A.0.1) that

\begin{equation} \cos(2A) \equiv \cos^2(A)-\sin^2(A) \equiv 2\cos^2(A) -1 \equiv 1-2\sin^2(A).\tag{A.0.13} \end{equation}

And hence,

\begin{equation} \cos^2(A) \equiv \frac{1+\cos(2A)}{2}, \qquad \sin^2(A) \equiv \frac{1-\cos(2A)}{2}\tag{A.0.14} \end{equation}
